WASP Warfare Build 87

BLUFOR and OPFOR fight for control of the whole map while GUER holds towns and harasses both armies. Capture towns, build factories, research upgrades, expand the economy, and destroy the enemy HQ. Each main side is led by a player or AI commander who controls the base, upgrades, and battlefield priorities.

Resources

Cash buys weapons, units, vehicles, support calls, and gear. Both main sides start with $30,000.
Supply is the commander's side pool for factories, structures, defences, and upgrades. Both main sides start with 12,800 supply.
Captured towns feed recurring +SV income, so taking and holding ground pays for the war.

Capturing Towns

Take the strongpoint bunkers around a town first, then hold numerical superiority at the town depot to capture it. Captured strongpoints become respawn points while your side controls the town area.
Capture grace period: after capture, enemy AI cannot respawn there for 5 minutes, and lingering enemy defenders have 3 minutes to clear before the timer expires.

Build 87 - Headline Changes

Command, Victory and AI Pressure

The commander deploys the HQ, builds and sells structures, researches upgrades, and can use the Command Center / War Room for remote purchases, artillery, and tactical support. Win by destroying the enemy HQ and collapsing their ability to recover, while the AI commander keeps autonomous teams moving through towns, relief, and HQ-strike pressure.

Air Cavalry, Airlift and Special Aircraft

Build 87 adds more air mobility and aircraft polish: air cavalry, vehicle airlift, AH-6X scout and gunship options, Ka-137 flare and swarm behaviour, and a broader EASA air roster where enabled. Use aircraft to scout, shift vehicles, hit weak fronts, and punish isolated pushes.

SCUD, ICBM and Tactical Strikes

SCUD and ICBM support now lives in the Tactical menu where enabled. The SCUD path separates conventional platform strikes from the later nuclear strike tier, with server-side validation for platform ownership, funds, cooldowns, and launch survival.

Bank (endgame objective)

The commander can build the Federal Reserve / Bank Rossii (9,500 supply; must be placed at least 800 m from your HQ/base area, one per side). While your HQ stands it pays a $6,000 pool every 5 minutes split among living players. Destroying the enemy bank pays +10,000 side supply and $25,000 to the killer. Both banks are marked on the map for both sides.

Counter Battery Radar (CBR)

Buildable structure (2,400 supply) that detects enemy artillery firing positions and marks them for 75 seconds. Requires your Anti-Air Radar to be alive. The CBR Radar upgrade extends detection radius: 750 m -> 1,500 m -> 2,000 m. Each captured airfield provides a permanent 2,000 m CBR.

Airfields

NWAF, NEAF and Balota Airfield are live capture objectives with heavy garrisons. Capturing an airfield gives your side a repair point, permanent CBR coverage for that field, and an exclusive hangar with aircraft unavailable elsewhere (L-39, An-2, Mi-17 variants on Chernarus).

Capture-to-Unlock Premium Units

Holding Krasnostav unlocks the Czech T-72 (Heavy Factory level 4, $7,000).
Holding NW Airfield unlocks the RM-70 rocket artillery (Light Factory level 4, $6,800; fully integrated into artillery fire missions).
Both unlock for the holding side only and are available at your own factories while you hold the trigger town.

Patrols and Convoys

Research the Patrols upgrade (4 levels; supply cost 300 / 1,600 / 2,400 / 3,200). Patrols spawn near your HQ and push toward the frontline, capturing as they go. Maximum 2 active patrols per side (1 at high population).
Level 4 Convoys: each patrol fields a supply truck that pays your team $750 split equally at every town stop.
Running patrols reduces every player's max AI count by 1.

Factory Queue and Buy Menu

Queue depth is shown as N/CAP in the buy menu. Caps scale with factory level but have minimum floors: Barracks 10, Light Factory 5, Heavy/Aircraft 3. You can cancel the last queued order for a partial refund. Special vehicle rows include hints and colour coding so players can read what they are buying before they commit.

Medic Redeployment Truck

Purchasable at the Light Factory (violet row). Medics only - acts as a forward spawn point when parked with the engine off, a free cargo seat, and at least 500 m from any non-friendly town.

GUER and Wildcards

GUER is a living third faction with no base of its own. It holds towns, escalates as it is pushed back, fields mechanized patrols, uses air defence and mortar tricks where available, and can swing the front with wildcard battlefield events.

Classes

Your class and its abilities are shown on join and are re-readable via the Class Info action in your action menu. Class tags (SOL/SUP/MED/ENG/SNI) appear on the map and in the Notes tab. Engineers repair and salvage, medics keep teams moving, specops and spotters scout and disrupt, and soldiers bring the largest AI team size.

Other Changes

  • EASA loadout editor tags each loadout [AA], [AG] or [MR] (multirole) so you know what you are switching to.
  • Rearm cost is proportional to ammo missing (10 % floor; artillery exempt).
  • Walls, defences, fortifications, statics and mines are budgeted per base area, with enemy proximity checks to prevent panic-building under direct pressure.
  • WDDM commander compositions are capped at 3 per base area. Over-cap attempts refund the cash.
  • Tanks and wheeled APCs come crewed by engineers.
  • Earplugs toggle now also fades radio and voice volume, and works while mounted in a vehicle.
  • Chat tips, class hints, map labels and telemetry polish make the current build easier to read in long rounds.
