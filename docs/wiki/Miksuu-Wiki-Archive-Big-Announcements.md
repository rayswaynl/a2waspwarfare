## Exploring Multithreading for Enhanced Performance
Our journey towards optimizing game performance has led us to explore the potential of multithreading. Here's an update on our progress and plans in this area.

### The Challenge with the New Server
- **Unexpected Server Performance:** The new server didn't perform as anticipated. This prompted me to experiment with multithreading during the holiday season to see if it could enhance the game's performance.

### Headless Clients: A Solution in Progress
- **Current Setup:** We've been using a single headless client, Mr. James, to offload processing.
- **Server Load Analysis:** On the new server, with 15 players, active towns, and a significant AI presence, we observed that the headless client's thread was maxed out at 100%, causing the server FPS to drop to 20, adversely affecting client FPS.
- **AI Count Adjustments:** We're considering tuning the AI count to balance performance. This might include modifying town defense counts. Your input on this will be crucial, so look out for upcoming vote suggestions and share your thoughts in the forum.

### Multithreading Implementation
- **Connecting Multiple Headless Clients:** I successfully connected four headless clients to the server. They appear to be efficiently distributing the load across the server cores, as indicated by CPU metrics and logs from the virtual machines. This approach utilizes 5 of the server's 6 cores, each delivering strong single-threaded performance.
- **Future Plans:** If this method proves effective, we may develop the mission even support more threads and invest in a server with a higher core count. However, we're aware that performance scaling may not be linear, so continuous testing and monitoring will be essential.
- **Development Branch:** A specialized branch has been created for this feature. Once it's ready for testing, we'll conduct a thorough playtest.
- **Playtesting:** Initially, testing will occur on Chernarus. Once we ensure stability, we'll implement the feature on other maps.
- **Identifying and Resolving Issues:** We've pinpointed the cause of malfunctions in town and static defenses when using multiple headless clients and are working on fixes.
- **Timeframe:** The development process could span from a few days to several months, depending on how smoothly we can resolve basic errors, and how the first playtest goes.
- **Old Architecture Challenges:** Given the complex and dated architecture of Arma 2, there's always a risk of encountering insurmountable obstacles. While we hope this won't be the case, such challenges might lead to temporary suspension of the feature.
- **Commitment to Improvement:** Despite potential difficulties, we are committed to pursuing this avenue, given the substantial performance benefits it could bring to our gaming experience.

Thank you for your continued support and patience as we navigate these technical challenges. Your feedback and suggestions are invaluable as we strive to enhance our game's performance.
